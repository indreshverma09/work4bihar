Agriculture

Out of Bihar’s total population, a substantial 75% has made farming as their occupation. A huge portion of these farmers lacks the money to buy basic farming resources such as seeds and manure. Work for Bihar aims to expand its agriculture to a state-level with an unprecedented approach which will constitute reinforcing traditional farming methods, an upswing in poultry and fisheries, low-cost products through various govt and non-govt channels, a robust supply chain to benefit the farmers directly and much more.

Our intentions outweigh the long ordeal of Bihar’s farmers who are not literate and are paying a lot more for purchasing farming-necessary items at inflated prices. We will take the agriculture industry head-on by supporting economic growth by enforcing a strict supply chain measure so that the farmers pay only what would be a just price. Though the Bihar government is emphasizing on various aspects of support to the economy and people alike, the farmers are still treated as the downtrodden and are exploited whenever people in power get a chance.

Bihar is deprived of streamlined agriculture. With an area of 93.6 lakh hectares out of which a majority of the area is utilized for tilling and growing crops, the whole process is still somewhat devoid of sustainable growth. The hurdles that are a deterrent in agriculture growth could be attributed to irregular rainfall, heterogeneity in landscape and fragmented landholdings. The other striking and impactful factor that has had Bihar struggle with agriculture is its division into northern and southern Bihar due to the river Ganges. With an excess of floods in the north and droughts in the south, Bihar has unable to bring equilibrium in its supply chain.

Work For Bihar intend to lend a helping hand by
  • Enhancing traditional farming towards the next level through fervent efforts.
  • Help increase the production in Poultry & Fisheries.
  • Helping the farmers find vital products at the lowest price.
  • Bringing agri start-ups at farmers’ doorstep.
  • Improving Economic Growth by Strengthening Agriculture and Investment Climate.
  • Use of modern technology of Irrigation.
  • Promotion of Organic products and sell and supply.
  • Set Centralize Gobar Gas in Rural Area and convert into a supply chain based mechanism.
  • Delocalized clustering, & integration of villages through a robust Hitech supply chain for agricultural produce on a Self-sustainable model.
  • Harnessing heritage & cultural tourism, Medical, & Education.
  • Bolstering Agri, food processing & related MSME sectors that are key to the development of Bihar where 90% population still lives in villages.
