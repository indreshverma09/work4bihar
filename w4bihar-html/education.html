Education

In a survey by Annual Status of Education Report (ASER), more than 50% of the students in 5th standard attending rural schools are not capable of reading a second standard textbook and are unable to solve basic mathematical questions. This is a stark reality in Bihar too where children are deprived of education due to distance, poverty or have to quit to sustain their families by leaving Bihar and heading to metro cities to make a living.

Most of Biharâ€™s people are peasants, farmers or labours who make around 3000-7000 a month which is a drastic sum especially when the prices of food and necessary household expenses have significantly inflated in 2020. Bihar lacks in education by a higher order of magnitude as no credible university or education society is present to help students to attain the best education.Bihar is in dire need of appropriate primary education by reinforcing their syllabus and school building to harbour the best talents in each city in Bihar.

The development of institutions such as schools, colleges, libraries will give way to employment as well because it will lead to the opening of other shops that will serve these institutions by providing them with food, books, hotels and more. Local transportation is also a major deterrent in travels to school and other places which have also hindered students from giving up education. Lack of coaching institutions, a billion-dollar industry in India, make students leave Bihar and head on to other states for studying properly wherein more money has to be spent by them in travelling and out of home expenses as opposed to the comfort and security of their own city.

We, therefore, intend to see a Bihar where no one would ever say that it is a backward land where no innovation or tech advances occur.
