Skill development

Bihar is the third largest state by population (10.41 crore as per 2011 Census). About 58 per cent of the population is below the age of 25, the highest in India in this age group. The literacy rate in Bihar is 63.8 per cent, with male literacy at 73.4 per cent and female literacy at 53.3 per cent.

According to the 2011 Census, about 31 per cent of the total population of Bihar is in the age group of less than 15 years and 61 per cent of them falls in the working-age group of 15-59 years.   • Creation of a skill development value chain.
  • Inclusion of people from all walks of life for imparting education and skills.
  • Launching of training programmes for both uneducated and educated.
  • Formulate trade strategies for maximizing employment opportunities.
  • Facilitate a coordinated approach in achieving the above objectives for maximizing the benefits over time and space and in penetrating to the neediest and helpless sections of the population.
